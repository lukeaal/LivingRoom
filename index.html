Competed at HackHarvard 2023 in a team of four awesome engineers and designers! We built GreenTrail, an app that grades hiking trails based on metrics from users' biometric data while performing the trail. GreenTrail allows anyone to view trails and accurately assess their physical preparedness. By aggregating past users' HR, BP, perspiration, and other data while on the trail from their wearables, we create distributions from which to compare others. We used TerraAPI, connecting to any wearable device to collect health and fitness data. The app, built in SwiftUI, uses Firebase for user and image storage. For design, Figma was utilized.

NumPy Only Neural Network üß†üç∑ - Wine Dataset

Wine region classification achieved by building a neural network from scratch using a Multi-Layer Perceptron algorithm. The implementation is based on 'Machine Learning: An Algorithmic Perspective' by Stephen Marsland, Chapman & Hall. The entire model is implemented in NumPy without the use of pandas or any other machine learning libraries. Although this model does not replace the expertise of a sommelier, it achieves an impressive accuracy of 96% in 5-fold cross-validation with just one hidden layer.

Pinhole üåòüåä- Live Exhibited Work, light and motion interactive

(IUB Science Fest 2024 - live exhibit)

(screen capture, Music - ‚ÄúSilhouette‚Äù by Pastel Ghost)

"Pinhole‚Äù is meant to mimic the pinhole phenomena during an eclipse where the pinholes of light that poke through a tree's canopy cast an image of the sun onto the ground bellow. Through visual distortion, the viewers form is instantiated multiple times and presented back to them just like the crescent shapes act as copies of the eclipse. In this sense, the viewer is the moon. As an added interactivity, ‚ÄúPinhole‚Äù reflects the change of light to dark during the eclipse; blocking all visible light from the performative camera changes the piece into a flurry of lighting bugs. While with full light, the view is of the users self and a dandelion blowing in the wind loosing its seeds.

Remix and Rememory ‚è≥üåå- Live Audio Reactive, for live music sets

(screen capture, Music - ‚ÄúGRAVERAISER‚Äù by Hezekaust)

"Remix and Rememory" consists of three multimedia scenes. Scene 1 depicts a black swirling galaxy ball expanding to engulf the entire frame, inspired by old video game aesthetics. Scene 2 shows hands partially submerged in remixed lava, with visible watermark stains representing appropriation in remix culture. The frame diminishes as noise increases. Scene 3 abandons any semblance of a frame, allowing incoherent noise to overtake coherent shapes and objects overlaid on the visuals. The scenes explore chaos, manipulation, and acceptance within online remix culture through stylistic choices like synchronized audio-visuals and fading effects on the hands. The piece contemplates complexities of digital creativity.
